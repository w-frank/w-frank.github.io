Mulches and mulching

What is mulching?

Mulches are materials added to surface of the soil. To be effective the material needs to be laid thickly. Most can be applied at any time, as long as the soil is moist. If the purpose is for insulation then the mulch should be applied after the soil has warmed up in Spring. Perennial weeds should be removed prior mulching and material should be kept away from the base of woody stems to avoid softening, and help to prevent diseases.

Benefits of mulching

Mulching can provide one or more of the following benefits:

  • Decorative finish (e.g. chippec bark on borders)
  • Supress weeds
  • Retain mosture
  • Protect the soil surface and reduce erosion
  • Maintain and increase soil organic matter
  • Encourage benefical soil organisms
  • Modify soil temperatures
  • Protect edible crops from contact with the soil
